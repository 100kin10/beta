At the 2011 Power Suit & Clapping Convention—aka The State Of The Union address—President Obama challenged the nation to produce 100,000 excellent new science, technology, engineer, and math (STEM) teachers by the year 2021. Unlike the majority of things promised by elected officials, this particular commitment actually resulted in, you know, something happening.

The reason for this plea: the United States hasn’t exactly been the sharpest bulb in the knife shed when it comes to international rankings in math and science. Which means we either start putting more great teachers into our nation’s classrooms or we invent some entirely new rankings system. But even that would require a crap ton of math, so the teaching option seems like our best bet.

Since Obama issued his 2011 challenge, the organization 100Kin10 has rallied hundreds of companies, advocates, non-profits, and teacher-prep programs around a single shared mission. In an era of fierce corporate competition and stark party-line politics, this movement to improve STEM education and recruit more teachers stands as a pretty remarkable example of collaboration in the name of the greater good.
