— Unspecified But Absolutely Real And Very Important Person

At the 2011 Flag Pin & Clapping Convention—aka The State Of The Union address—President Obama challenged the nation to produce 100,000 excellent new science, technology, engineering, and math (STEM) teachers by the year 2021. Unlike the majority of things called for by elected officials, this particular commitment actually resulted in, you know, something happening.

The reason for this plea: the United States hasn’t exactly been the sharpest bulb in the knife shed when it comes to international rankings in math and science. That means we either start putting more great teachers into our nation’s classrooms, or we invent some entirely new rankings system. But even that would require a bunch of hard math and stuff, so the teaching option seems like our best bet.

Since President Obama issued his 2011 challenge, the organization 100Kin10 has rallied scores of foundations, universities, nonprofits, and other partners around a single shared mission. In an era of fierce corporate competition and stark party-line politics, this movement to improve STEM education and recruit more teachers stands as a pretty remarkable example of collaboration in the name of the greater good.

How can you help?

For starters, have you ever considered teaching STEM?



Maybe you already are a teacher? Or maybe you like your job, but also believe in this mission? Or maybe you have a weird about-page fetish, and now you wanna take this to the next level? Whatever it may be, the least you can do is share the living crap out of this site, our animation, and anything else you find interesting, funny, or worthy of your precious social platforms.

Then do one more thing not normally part of the digital sharing experience: think. Hard. About who you know that this campaign might speak to. A friend, a niece, a neighbor, the weirdo reading this over your shoulder.

If you know someone who has studied STEM—who knows firsthand how mind blowing those subjects can be—and they’re maybe curious about what to do with their degree, direct them to this site. It’s okay if you tell them we forced you.
